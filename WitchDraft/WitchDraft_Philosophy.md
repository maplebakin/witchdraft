# WitchDraft Design Philosophy

> *"Writing isn't creating characters and stories. It's listening to the stories that characters want to tell you."*
> — Mira Ashe

---

## The Core Truth

WitchDraft is not a writing app with a witchy aesthetic.

It is a **divination engine for story.**

The workflow of an intuitive writer and the workflow of a skilled reader of signs are identical:

1. **Cast widely** — beats, impressions, character fragments, images, feelings without names yet
2. **Hold loosely** — let them sit in the pool without forcing meaning or order
3. **Find the pattern** — connections emerge, arcs reveal themselves, the shape of the story surfaces
4. **Read it** — drill down into what the pattern is telling you the story needs to be

Every feature in WitchDraft exists to support one of these four modes. Nothing else belongs here.

---

## The Divination Metaphor as Design Law

The **Beats pool** is the spread — raw potential, unconstrained, waiting to be read.

The **Constellation** is the reading — the pattern made visible, the relationships between elements illuminated.

The **Character cards** are the significators — not static profiles but living presences with histories, trajectories, and intentions of their own.

The **Hearth** is the sacred space where the listening actually happens.

A skilled reader does not throw all the cards at once and demand you interpret them simultaneously. They **draw what is needed, when it is needed.** Everything else waits.

This is the law the UI must obey.

---

## The Writer This Tool Is Built For

This writer does not outline top-down. They **gather** first.

Story beats arrive before chapter containers exist for them. Characters announce themselves before their arcs are understood. Meaning crystallizes around what has been caught, not around a predetermined structure.

This writer also has **ADHD** and processes entirely in **language** — not images, not spatial maps, but words and patterns of words. The tool must reduce cognitive load at every turn. Friction is the enemy of the listening state.

The entry point must be **singular and obvious.** The depth must be **always available but never imposing.**

Opening WitchDraft should feel like sitting down in a quiet room with a candle. Not like walking into a dashboard.

## The Four Modes & What They Need

### 1. Cast — Free Capture
*"I have an idea and I need to put it somewhere before it disappears."*

- Frictionless. One action, no decisions required.
- Beats captured globally — no chapter assignment needed yet.
- Characters sketched in fragments — a name, a voice, a feeling.
- Nothing needs to be categorized to be saved.

### 2. Hold — The Pool
*"I have things gathered. I'm not ready to place them yet."*

- The beats pool and character fragments exist without demanding structure.
- No hierarchy imposed. No pressure to connect yet.
- The writer can browse, tag loosely, let it breathe.

### 3. Pattern — The Constellation
*"Something is emerging. Let me see what it looks like."*

- Relationships between beats, characters, and themes become visible.
- The Constellation renders the map of what has been caught.
- Character cards surface their arc as it actually exists — beats they're tagged in, past and intended.
- This is where the story reveals its own shape.

### 4. Read — The Hearth
*"I know what I'm writing toward. Let me listen."*

- The writing surface is the whole world.
- Checked beats for this chapter float quietly as reference — a loose map, not a cage.
- Character cards accessible on demand — pull up a significator mid-session.
- Everything else recedes completely.

---

## UI Law: The Three Tiers

**Tier 1 — Always Present**
The Hearth. Chapter title. Word count. Save state. That is all.
This tier never moves, never crowds, never competes.

**Tier 2 — One Gesture Away**
Chapter list. Beats pool. Outliner. Storylines.
Navigation and structure. Summoned when needed, dismissed cleanly.

**Tier 3 — Intentional Reach**
Constellation. Character cards. Notes. Sparks. Companion Doc. Exhale.
Deep work panels. Never open by default. Always available. Always respectful of the listening state.

## The Beats System

Beats are the heart of the pre-structure layer.

A Beat is:
- A story moment, image, line, emotional note, or plot point
- **Global by default** — not assigned to a chapter until the writer is ready
- **Character-tagged** — one or more characters can be attached to a beat
- **Status-tracked** — Idea → Active → Used (composted)

When writing a chapter, the writer can **pull up the beats pool and check off** which beats they're working toward in this session. Checked beats become a quiet reference layer — visible but unobtrusive.

Beats are never deleted. Used beats are **composted** — filed away, still accessible, honoring the journey.

---

## The Character Card System

A character is not a form to fill out. A character is a **presence to listen to.**

A Character Card holds:
- **Profile** — personality, voice, physical presence, role in the story world
- **Past beats** — beats tagged to this character that are marked Used
- **Active beats** — beats tagged to this character currently in play
- **Intended beats** — beats tagged to this character still in the Idea pool

The arc is **never manually curated.** It assembles itself from the beats the writer has already captured. The character card is a reading of what is already true.

Cards are accessible mid-session as a Tier 3 pull — summon a character, read their current arc, return to the Hearth.

---

## What Does Not Belong Here

- Gamification beyond Sparks (session-scoped, low-stakes)
- Productivity metrics or accountability features
- Anything that makes the writer feel behind
- Anything that imposes structure before the writer is ready for it
- Visual noise that competes with the listening state

---

## The Name

**WitchDraft.**

A draft is a first listening. A witch is someone who knows how to read what is already there.

This tool is for writers who already know the story is real before they've written a word of it. It holds the space for that knowing to become language.
